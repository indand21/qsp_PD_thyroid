# Supplementary Mathematical Methods

## Expanded 19-State QSP Model of ICI-Induced Thyroid Dysfunction: Computational and Statistical Methods

**Supplementary material for:** *An Expanded Quantitative Systems Pharmacology
Model of Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor-Induced Thyroid Dysfunction: A Calibrated
In-Silico Framework.*

> All results are in-silico. No real patient data were used. Cohorts, biomarker
> trajectories, and validation datasets are generated by the mechanistic model.

---

## 1. Scope and relationship to the equations document

The complete mathematical specification of the mechanistic model - the 19-state
vector, pharmacokinetics, receptor occupancy, all five module ODEs, initial
conditions, thyroid-status classification, the susceptibility mixture, parameter
tables, and the numerical integrator - is given in
`QSP_Mathematical_Equations_Documentation.md`. This document specifies the
**computational and statistical methods** built around that model: calibration,
epidemiological endpoints, global sensitivity analysis, the virtual population,
synthetic cohorts, predictive validation, and the machine-learning ensemble. It
does not restate the ODEs. Every procedure below is implemented in `qsp_expanded/`
and `scripts/`, orchestrated by `scripts/run_v1_pipeline.py` at the scale set in
`config/v1_pipeline.yaml`.

## 2. Pharmacokinetics, occupancy, and the mechanistic core

Each drug is represented by an analytic one-compartment multi-dose PK profile;
concentrations drive a concentration-dependent receptor-occupancy equilibrium per
axis (PD-1, CTLA-4), which enters the T-cell module. See Sections 3-9 of the
equations document. Anti-PD-1 and anti-PD-L1 agents act on the PD-1 axis;
ipilimumab on the CTLA-4 axis; the combination drives both. Combination synergy
is encoded explicitly (not fitted) through a Treg-synergy term by which CTLA-4
blockade weakens regulatory T-cell suppression of effector expansion.

## 3. Patient susceptibility and covariate model

Overall autoimmune susceptibility $\phi$ is sampled from a two-component
lognormal mixture (high-risk fraction $\pi = 0.092$; equations document Section
11). Virtual patients additionally carry covariates - age, sex, HLA-DQ risk,
baseline TSH, and baseline TPOAb propensity - drawn from a covariate model and
mapped onto $\phi$ and the autoantibody drive, plus lognormal between-subject
random effects on selected mechanistic parameters (a Python nonlinear
mixed-effects-style sampler; `qsp_expanded/population.py`,
`scripts/population/virtual_population.py`). This mixture is the mechanism that
decouples incidence (which patients cross the disease threshold) from onset
timing (how fast they cross), together with the always-active damage
repair/turnover term.

## 4. Calibration

Susceptibility-mixture, onset/damage, per-drug potency, the CTLA-4 effector
weight, and the Treg-synergy coefficient are tuned by staged calibration so that
180-day per-drug hypothyroidism incidence falls within published acceptance
ranges, with the target class ordering and a 60-140 day onset window. Calibration
targets and acceptance ranges (per-drug incidence) are literature-anchored;
onset-timing parameters set the crossing time, the high-risk fraction sets the
incidence level, per-drug potency sets the between-drug spread, and the CTLA-4
effector weight together with the Treg-synergy coefficient sets the
CTLA-4-monotherapy-versus-combination balance. Calibration is verified over
independent simulated populations
(`scripts/calibration/calibrate_expanded_model.py`,
`results/v1/calibration_report.json`). The calibration report is a verification
draw; the reported epidemiological endpoints (Section 5) are computed in the
incidence stage from a single simulated population so that incidence and onset
are reported from the same draw.

## 5. Incidence, onset, and risk-hierarchy statistics

For each drug a population of $n$ virtual patients is simulated to 180 days; each
patient's trajectory is classified per time point (equations document Section
12). A patient is a hypothyroidism / hyperthyroidism case if that status occurs
at any time; median hypothyroid onset is the median first-crossing day among
affected patients, reported from the same population as the incidence. Per-rate
95% confidence intervals use the Wilson (score) method. Across mechanistic
classes (PD-1, PD-L1, CTLA-4, Combination) a chi-square test of the
class-by-hypothyroidism contingency table is reported, and the relative risk of
hypothyroidism for PD-1 vs. PD-L1 with a log-method 95% CI
(`scripts/analysis/class_incidence.py`).

## 6. Biphasic dynamics and seroconversion

Biphasic cases are those in which a hyperthyroid (thyrotoxic) phase precedes a
hypothyroid phase. For each class, the proportion of dysfunction cases that are
biphasic and the median thyrotoxic-onset, hypothyroid-onset, and
transition-gap weeks are reported over the affected subset
(`scripts/analysis/biphasic_temporal.py`). New-onset autoantibody seroconversion
(TPOAb, TgAb) is defined by titer crossing fixed thresholds during treatment,
with median seroconversion week per class
(`scripts/analysis/seroconversion.py`). Reported percentages are accompanied by
their denominators (number of dysfunction cases per class), which are small for
low-incidence classes.

## 7. Global sensitivity analysis

Morris one-at-a-time screening and Sobol variance decomposition (SALib) are
computed over ten mechanistic parameters spanning the T-cell, cytokine, thyroid,
and HPT modules (patient susceptibility, damage rate, PD-1 and CTLA-4 effector
weights, IFN production, Treg suppression, thyrocyte death, regeneration, IFN
EC50 for death, and T4-to-T3 conversion). The deterministic output is a bounded
composite thyroid-dysfunction index,

$$
J = 0.6\,(1 - Thyro(180)) + 0.4\,\mathrm{clip}\!\left(\frac{\max_t TSH - 1.5}{48.5},\,0,\,1\right),
$$

evaluated for a nivolumab exposure. Morris $\mu^{\ast}/\sigma$ and Sobol
first-order ($S_1$) and total-order ($S_T$) indices are reported
(`scripts/sensitivity/global_sensitivity_analysis.py`). Because susceptibility is
a direct multiplier on damage drive, its dominance is expected by construction.

## 8. Virtual population and risk stratification

A virtual population of covariate-sampled patients (Section 3) is simulated to
12 months. Population incidence is reported with a Monte-Carlo bootstrap 95% CI.
Patients are stratified into mechanistic risk tiers by their model
dysfunction-probability at fixed cut points (high $\ge 0.60$, intermediate
$\ge 0.30$, low otherwise). Because the tiers are defined on model outcome
probability, which is itself driven by the input susceptibility mixture, the
resulting near-bimodal outcome structure reflects the imposed mixture rather than
an independent emergent finding (`scripts/population/virtual_population.py`).

## 9. Synthetic cohorts

Two synthetic cohorts (a calibration cohort and an independent external cohort
whose "centers" are distinct covariate strata) are generated by mapping sampled
covariates onto model parameters, simulating each virtual patient to 12 months,
and recording serial thyroid biomarkers (TSH, total T4, total T3, TPOAb, TgAb)
with lognormal measurement noise, a baseline serum cytokine panel, and the
dysfunction outcome (`scripts/cohorts/generate_synthetic_cohorts.py`). Cohort
sizes are fixed in configuration; the covariate sampling yields an event rate
modestly above the marginal calibrated incidence (mild risk enrichment), and the
classes remain imbalanced as in the source population. The baseline cytokine
panel is a serum-assay feature set and is not restricted to the four cytokines
carried as ODE states.

## 10. Predictive validation

A baseline-feature logistic model is evaluated by a 70/30 split and by 10-fold
repeated stratified cross-validation (internal), then applied to the external
cohort with sensitivity, specificity, predictive values, and a calibration slope
(`scripts/validation/internal_external_validation.py`). A visual predictive check
compares observed TSH against the model's simulated prediction interval. Because
cohorts are model-generated, these metrics characterize internal consistency and
learnability, not real-world accuracy; the fixed cohort sizes give a limited
number of events, so predictive-value estimates are unstable and are interpreted
with that caveat.

## 11. Machine-learning ensemble

A four-algorithm ensemble - logistic regression, random forest, XGBoost, and
LightGBM - predicts 12-month dysfunction from early-treatment features (baseline
covariates and antibodies, a baseline cytokine panel, drug-class indicators, and
on-treatment biomarkers through week 12). Hyperparameters for the gradient-boosted
component (XGBoost) are tuned by Optuna (tree-structured Parzen estimator); the
other algorithms use fixed settings. Performance is summarized by repeated
cross-validated AUC and external-cohort AUC
(`scripts/ml/ensemble_classifier.py`). Univariate logistic odds ratios (internal
and external) are reported for baseline TPOAb positivity, HLA-DQ risk, baseline
IL-17, and week-12 TSH (`scripts/analysis/biomarker_odds_ratios.py`); binary
predictors that induce the same partition in a cohort yield identical estimates
and represent a single signal.

## 12. Numerical methods and reproducibility

All ODE integration uses SciPy `solve_ivp` with LSODA (rtol $10^{-6}$, atol
$10^{-9}$, `max_step` 1.0 day; automatic looser-tolerance fallback), as in the
equations document Section 13. The entire pipeline is seeded
(`config/v1_pipeline.yaml`) and reproducible via `scripts/run_v1_pipeline.py`;
the configuration records the simulation scale (patients per drug, virtual-
population size, Monte-Carlo depth, sensitivity sample sizes, and ML
cross-validation/hyperparameter-optimization budgets). Software: Python with
NumPy, SciPy, pandas, scikit-learn, statsmodels, SALib, XGBoost, LightGBM,
Optuna, and Matplotlib.
